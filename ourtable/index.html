The Problem

Cooking for a household is a coordination problem

This app started because of my wife. She's a passionate home cook who saves recipes from TikTok and Instagram — and then can't find them when she needs them. Screenshots buried in camera roll, links in DMs that disappear, recipes saved to five different apps with no connection between them.

But the bigger problem isn't recipe management — it's that cooking for a household involves multiple people with different dietary needs, different schedules, and a shared fridge nobody has a full picture of. No app solves this as a household coordination problem. They all solve it as an individual food tracker.

🤌

Recipes live everywhere

TikTok, Instagram, screenshots, links in messages — no unified home for saved recipes that actually stays organized.

🧑‍🍳

Cooking for more than one person

No app thinks about the household as the unit. Who's eating tonight? What does the fridge actually have? What can we make?

Core Features

Five screens that solve five real problems

📸

Save from Screenshot

Photograph a TikTok recipe, Instagram post, or any screen. OurTable parses the image and saves a clean, structured recipe automatically.

🧊

AI Fridge Scanner

Open the camera, point at your fridge. The AI catalogues what's there. Tap "What can we make?" and it surfaces recipes matching your inventory.

🛒

Smart Grocery List

Add items manually or pull ingredients directly from a recipe. Items auto-categorize by section (produce, dairy, pantry). Check off as you shop.

📖

Shared Recipe Book

A household-shared library. Both partners see the same saved recipes. No more "what did you save that pasta one?" — it's in the book.

🌙

Light & Dark Mode

Both modes designed from day one. Dark for late-night cooking in a dim kitchen. Light for daytime browsing.

⚠️

Dietary Conflict Warnings

Set preferences per household member. If a recipe contains an ingredient that conflicts with someone's diet, OurTable flags it and suggests a swap.

Design Decisions

Why it looks and works the way it does

→
Terracotta + Cream — not the usual food app green

Most recipe apps reach for fresh green. OurTable uses terracotta (#C4614A) and warm cream (#FAF7F0) because they feel like a kitchen — warm, lived-in, the colors of cast iron and wooden cutting boards. The palette is deliberate positioning away from "health app" and toward "home cooking."

→
Playfair Display for recipe titles, Inter for UI

Recipe names and dish titles use Playfair Display — an editorial serif that makes a recipe feel special, not like a database entry. All navigation and UI labels stay in Inter. Two fonts, clear hierarchy, no confusion about what's content and what's chrome.

→
Household as the primary unit, not the individual

Every design decision flows from this. The recipe book is shared. The grocery list is shared. The fridge scanner feeds the whole household's context. Individual profiles exist but they're inside a household — not the other way around.

→
Fridge scanner → recipe suggestions is the killer feature

This is what nobody else has. "What can I make with what I have?" is the most common question before cooking. Pointing a camera at a fridge and getting a real answer closes a loop that every other app leaves open. It's the reason the fridge tab gets its own bottom nav slot.

→
When the scan isn't confident, give users control

One challenge was handling situations where the AI couldn't confidently identify what was in the fridge. Rather than guessing and risking bad recipe recommendations, users can quickly review, remove, or add ingredients before moving forward. This keeps the experience accurate while giving users control when the scan isn't perfect.

→
Grocery list check-off uses sage green, not the primary color

Checked items turn sage green (#7A8C52) and get a line-through. The terracotta primary stays reserved for CTAs and active states. Sage green carries a "done, healthy, complete" semantic that terracotta doesn't — and it matches the food context without looking like a warning.
